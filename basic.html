Basic Automotive

Automotive Basics includes knowledge of the basic automotive systems and the theory and principles of the components that make up each system and how to service these systems. Automotive Basics includes applicable safety and environmental rules and regulations. In Automotive Basics, students will gain knowledge and skills in the repair, maintenance, and servicing of vehicle systems. This study allows students to reinforce, apply, and transfer academic knowledge and skills to a variety of interesting and relevant activities, problems, and settings. The focus of this course is to teach safety, tool identification, proper tool use, and employability.

The Engine: The Heart of the Car

The engine is the most vital part of a car. It is a complex machine that converts heat from burning gas into mechanical energy, which powers the car. The engine consists of various components, including pistons, camshafts, and crankshafts. There are different types of engines, such as gasoline and diesel engines, each with its own characteristics and fuel requirements. Understanding how the engine works is essential for any beginner car enthusiast.

The Transmission: Connecting Power to the Wheels

Understanding how the transmission works is essential for beginners delving into the world of automotive technology. The transmission is responsible for transferring the power generated by the engine to the wheels, allowing the car to move forward or backward. It consists of a series of gears that work together to control the speed and torque of the vehicle.

The Brakes: Keeping You Safe on the Road

When it comes to car maintenance, understanding the brakes is crucial for newcomers. The brakes play a critical role in ensuring your safety on the road by allowing you to slow down or stop your vehicle when necessary. Most modern cars are equipped with disc brakes, although some older models still use drum brakes. Let’s take a closer look at how the braking system works and some essential tips for maintaining it

The Suspension: Smooth and Stable Ride

Understanding the suspension system is crucial for beginners looking to grasp the basics of how cars work. The suspension plays a vital role in providing a comfortable and stable ride by absorbing bumps and vibrations, while also maintaining contact between the wheels and the road surface.

The Electrical System: Powering Your Car

The electrical system in a car is like the nervous system in the human body – it powers and controls various components, ensuring smooth operation and functionality. Understanding the basics of the electrical system is essential for beginners who want to have a good grasp of how their cars work.

The Cooling System: Preventing Overheating

The cooling system is a crucial component in any car, responsible for maintaining the engine’s temperature and preventing overheating. Understanding how the cooling system works is essential for beginners to ensure the optimal performance and longevity of their vehicles.

The Exhaust System: Removing Waste Gases

In a car, the exhaust system is responsible for removing waste gases generated during the combustion process. It plays a crucial role in maintaining engine performance and reducing harmful emissions. Understanding the basics of the exhaust system is important for beginners in automotive engineering and car mechanics.

The automotive industry is constantly evolving, and is currently undergoing a transformation with the introduction of new technologies like robotics, additive manufacturing, and the Internet of Things. These technologies are paving the way for the development of autonomous vehicles.

About

Automotive technology is the study of how self-propelled vehicles move, and includes the design, creation, and repair of vehicle technology. Automotive technicians and related careers work on a variety of vehicles, including consumer vehicles, public transportation, heavy machinery, and heavy trucks.

Some skills that automotive technicians develop include: engine construction, fuel and ignition, brakes, power trains, and electronic and diagnostic equipment
